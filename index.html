⚡ Light Wrangler

Full control over your light setup — positioning, intensity, color, and even the ability to swap light textures (gobos) from a library of presets. It manages which lights exist and how they behave.

🎨 Light Painter

Full control over the light texture itself — paint any gobo pattern you can imagine, exactly where you want it. It defines what the light projects and how it shapes the scene.

Light Wrangler gives you preset gobos and scene-wide light management. Light Painter gives you pixel-level creative freedom to craft any texture from scratch. Together, they cover every aspect of lighting — from broad scene setup to the finest artistic detail.

Deep controls for precision work

Under the hood, Gobo Painter offers advanced settings for light space, camera space, blur, edge protection, gradient mixing, surface targeting and more.

🎯 Light Space vs Camera Space

Choose whether your strokes are mapped relative to the light's own projection (Light Space) or the current camera view (Camera Space). Light Space ensures consistent results regardless of viewing angle; Camera Space lets you paint exactly what you see.

💨 Brush Blur

Apply a blur effect directly to your brush strokes. This softens hard edges and creates natural-looking light falloff — perfect for replicating real-world gobo diffusion without post-processing.

🛡️ Edge Leak Protection

Prevents your paint strokes from "bleeding" beyond the light's actual projection boundary. No more invisible painting outside the cone — the add-on clips your strokes to the active light area automatically.

🔀 Gradient Blend Modes

Mix gradient results with existing gobo content using Photoshop-style blend modes: Overlay, Soft Light, Multiply, Screen, and more. Stack multiple gradients with different modes for complex lighting patterns.

📐 Target Surface

Constrain your paint strokes to only affect areas where the light actually hits a selected mesh. This keeps your gobo edits precise and context-aware, avoiding wasted effort on areas outside your scene geometry.

👻 Skip Transparent

When enabled, your brush skips over fully transparent regions of existing layers, preserving areas you've intentionally left empty. Useful for painting refinements on complex multi-layer gobo setups.

Control the light.

Use familiar selection tools — rectangle, lasso, polyline, and brush — to mask regions of your gobo. Paint, fill, or apply gradients only where you need them, with pixel-accurate precision directly in the viewport.

▶   Selection Tools Demo

Without selections, every stroke affects the entire gobo. Selections let you isolate regions — add a hard light edge here, a soft wash there — all without touching the rest. Combined with layers, this gives you non-destructive, surgical control over every part of your light pattern.

04 — Layer System

Layers of light,
infinite possibilities

Build complex gobo textures from any number of independent layers — each with its own blend mode, transform controls, fill color or texture. Rearrange, toggle, and tweak without ever losing your work. A Photoshop-grade layer stack, built for light.

▶   Layer System Demo

The problem it solves: A single gobo texture is fragile — one wrong edit can ruin hours of work. Layers let you separate concerns: one layer for the base gradient, another for detail painting, a third for a texture overlay. Each layer supports blend modes (Overlay, Soft Light, Multiply, Screen and more) plus per-layer offset, rotation, and scale transforms. Non-destructive, flexible, and endlessly composable.

Animate

Keyframe your layers,
animate the light

Light Painter fully supports Blender's animation system. Add keyframes to any layer — or an entire set of layers — and watch your light textures animate smoothly over time. Move, fade, transform, and blend your gobo patterns across frames with full timeline control.

TIMELINE

The key detail: you never touch the light object itself. All animation happens at the texture layer level — the light stays exactly where it is while the painted pattern evolves. This gives you a clean separation between light placement and light design, making complex animated lighting setups far easier to manage.
